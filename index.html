TYPOGRAPHY

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type.
Typography is important because it conveys emotion. The choice of typeface can affect how a piece is understood. Typefaces can illicit all kinds of different feelings. From the serious Serifs to the minimal San Serifs, there's an appropriate type for every occasion.
How should you chose a typeface?
Start by asking yourself a few questions. What is the purpose of this typeface? What kind of audience am I trying to reach?
In general you should limit the typefaces of your product to as few as possible. Titles and headers can be more decorative but text that is meant to be read and consumed should be easy to track, serving as a utility function.

COLOR THEORY

Color theory is a body of practical guidance to color mixing and the visual effects of specific color combinations.

Color when used right can be your greatest design tool. The right color scheme is vital to creating the mood and feeling associated with a product. Here's a quick look at how a color wheel is divided up.

Color harmony can be achieved through understanding color wheel themes.

Analogous color schemes create a sense of harmony and congruity. It is often found in nature.

Secondary color schemes are evenly spaced around the color wheel. One color should be used as a base with the others as accents.

Complimentary color schemes are colors that are opposite on the color wheel. It creates a vibrant look especially at full saturation but can be very bad for text.

SPACE

Space or lack thereof can be used to make a point.

Space in Visual arts often referrs to white space or nagative space. It is an important element of design which enables the objects in it to exist. The balance between positive (or non-white) and the use of negative spaces is key to aesthetic composition.

A page crammed full of text or graphics with very little white space runs the risk of appearing busy or cluttered, and is typically difficult to read. On the other hand judicious use of negative space can give a page a classic, elegant, or rich appearance.

HEIARCHY

Good visual hierarchy isn't about wild and crazy graphics or the newest photoshop filters, it's about organizing information in a way that's usable, accessible, and logical to the everyday site visitor.

Here are a few tools used to create hierachy.

Size

Objects that are bigger demand more attention. Using size as a hierarchal tool is an effective way of guiding a viewer's eye to a particular portion of the page.

It's important to correlate size with importance in a design.

Contrast

Contrast shows relative important. Dramatic shifts in text-size or color will impart a message that something is different and requires attention.

Changing from a light background color to a dark background color can quickly separate the core content of a page from the footer.

Alignment

Alignment creates order between elements.

Users will generally look for information in sidebars and headers. This is a popular format in social media sites.
